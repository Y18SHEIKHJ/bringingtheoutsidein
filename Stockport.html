There are lots of ways of low-income households to bring the outside in. For example, you can get some air plants.

Advantages of Air Plants
 Toxins in the surrounding air are hazardous to human health, and children are particularly vulnerable. Plants that filter the air can help keep the amounts of these indoor air pollutants in check. Air plants have been discovered to absorb mercury from the air, according to one study.

They are cheap to get and you don't need much to look after them just water.

Another way low income-households can bring the outside in is to get some seeds.

Advantage of using Native Plants
 Native plants use less water than lawns and aid in erosion prevention. Many native Midwestern plants have deep root systems that boost the soil's ability to store water. Water runoff and, as a result, floods can be considerably reduced by native plants.
